Local Language Models: Architecture, Deployment, and Practical Considerations

Running language models locally provides full control over inference, complete
reproducibility, and independence from API rate limits and costs. The ecosystem of
open-weight models has matured rapidly, with several families offering strong performance
at sizes that fit on consumer GPUs.

The Llama Family (Meta)

Llama 3.1 (July 2024) introduced models at 8B, 70B, and 405B parameters. The 8B variant
is particularly significant for research: it offers strong instruction following and
reasoning at a modest footprint of approximately 4.7 GB in Q4 quantisation. Key
architectural choices include grouped-query attention (GQA) with 8 key-value heads shared
across 32 query heads, RoPE positional embeddings with a context length of 128K tokens,
and SwiGLU activations in the feed-forward layers. The training data included over 15
trillion tokens of publicly available text.

Llama 3.2 (September 2024) introduced smaller models at 1B and 3B parameters designed for
on-device and edge deployment. The 3B model achieves surprisingly strong performance at
approximately 2 GB in Q4 quantisation, making it viable on laptops and mobile devices. The
key trade-off is reduced reasoning depth — the 3B model can follow instructions well but
struggles with multi-step logical chains that the 8B handles comfortably. The 1B model
is primarily useful for simple classification and extraction tasks.

The Qwen Family (Alibaba)

Qwen 2.5 (September 2024) offers models at 0.5B, 1.5B, 3B, 7B, 14B, 32B, and 72B
parameters. The family is notable for excellent multilingual capability (29 languages)
and strong instruction adherence even at small sizes. The 7B variant is competitive with
Llama 3.1 8B on most benchmarks despite having fewer parameters, partly due to a larger
training corpus (18 trillion tokens) and improved tokenizer with better coverage of
non-English scripts. The 3B variant serves as an excellent cross-family comparison point
against Llama 3.2 3B, as both target the same parameter budget but differ in architecture,
training data, and tokenizer design.

The Gemma Family (Google)

Gemma 2 (June 2024) provides 2B and 9B variants trained on 8 trillion tokens of web data,
code, and mathematics. The 2B model is notable for its training efficiency — Google applied
aggressive knowledge distillation from larger internal models during pre-training, making
it one of the most capable models at its size class. The architecture uses multi-query
attention and RMSNorm, with a 8K default context window extensible to 32K.

The Phi Family (Microsoft)

Phi-3 (April 2024) represents a different approach to small model training. Rather than
training on web-scale data, Phi-3 was trained primarily on synthetic data generated by
GPT-4, plus carefully filtered web data. The 3.8B model achieves performance competitive
with models 2-3x its size on reasoning benchmarks. This makes it particularly interesting
for knowledge distillation research: Phi-3 is itself a product of distillation (through
synthetic data), so testing whether a distillation-born model has different self-knowledge
properties is a novel research question.

Local Deployment with Ollama

Ollama provides a simple runtime for running quantised models locally. It handles model
downloading, quantisation format conversion, memory management, and GPU offloading
automatically. Key commands: ollama pull llama3.1:8b downloads the model, ollama run
llama3.1:8b starts an interactive session, and the /api/generate endpoint provides
programmatic access. Running at temperature 0.0 ensures deterministic output, which is
essential for reproducible experiments. Models are stored in ~/.ollama/models/ and can
be managed with ollama list and ollama rm commands.

Quantisation and Memory Requirements

4-bit quantisation (Q4_K_M) reduces model size by approximately 4x compared to FP16 with
minimal quality degradation (typically <2% on benchmarks). A 3B model at Q4 requires
approximately 2 GB of VRAM, while an 8B model requires approximately 4.7 GB. The NVIDIA
RTX 3070 Ti (8 GB VRAM) can comfortably run an 8B teacher and a 3B student sequentially,
though not simultaneously. For simultaneous inference, the models must share the GPU
through Ollama's automatic memory management.
